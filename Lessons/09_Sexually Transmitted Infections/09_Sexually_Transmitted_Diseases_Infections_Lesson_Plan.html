Overview

This lesson focuses on learning about sexually transmitted infections, the most common methods of transmission, and the best way to prevent and reduce the risk of infection.

Learning Targets

  • Identify how STIs are spread.
  • Discuss the best way to prevent and reduce the risk of infections from an STI.
  • Analyze why it is important for children to get the HPV shot.
  • Recognize the short- and long-term consequences of STIs.

Warm-Up Activity

Select a warm-up activity to help get your class focused and on task.

  • Journal Question: Write out what STI stands for and list the STIs that you know.
    • Option: Write or project the question and have students respond in their journals as they enter class.
    • Option: Have students discuss the question with a partner or in a small group.
  • Vocabulary Review: Have students work individually, in pairs, or in small groups to complete the Sexually Transmitted Infections Vocabulary Review Worksheet.
  • Quiz: Have students complete the Sexually Transmitted Infections Quiz to assess their prior knowledge.
    • Option: Collect the quiz and use it alongside a posttest to demonstrate student learning.
    • Option: Have students share their answers with a partner and then go over the answers together as a class.

Reflection and Summary

Review the critical content from today’s lesson. Review the learning targets and ask students to answer each question posed.

Can you...

  • Identify how STIs are spread?

    Unprotected sexual activity.

  • Discuss the best way to prevent and reduce the risk of infections from an STI?

    Abstinence.

  • Analyze why it is important for children to get the HPV shot?

    To help protect children from developing any of the cancers from HPV.

  • Recognize the short- and long-term consequences of STIs?

    The short- and long-term consequences of STIs are infecting others if you don’t know you have an STI; becoming infertile, which means you either can’t get someone pregnant or become pregnant; have problems with a pregnancy such as a miscarriage, premature birth, or a stillbirth; and develop other complications from an untreated STI such as bladder problems, cervical cancer, anal cancer, liver cancer, blindness, dementia, and damage to the heart, brain, and bones.

Assessment

Complete one or more of the following assessment tasks for this lesson.

  • Quiz: Have students take the Sexually Transmitted Infections Quiz.
  • Vocabulary Review: Collect the Sexually Transmitted Infections Vocabulary Review Worksheets and evaluate them for accuracy.
  • Note-Taking Guide: Collect the completed Sexually Transmitted Infections Note-Taking Guide and spot-check one or more items for completion and accuracy.
  • Skill-Building Challenge Worksheet: Have students submit the Skill-Building Challenge Worksheet and use the Interpersonal Communication Holistic Rubric to evaluate their skill development.
  • Journal Question: Ask students to respond to the journal question again, adding information they learned from today’s class. Require a one-paragraph response that uses proper grammar.
